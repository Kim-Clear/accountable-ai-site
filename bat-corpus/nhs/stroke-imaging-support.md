## Workflow Summary

The NHS England Round 1 AI in Health and Care Awards page describes e-Stroke
Suite as tools using AI methods to interpret acute stroke brain scans, support
doctors in treatment and transfer decisions, and enable information sharing
between hospitals.

## What the AI Does

The AI interprets acute stroke brain scans and provides support for treatment
choice and specialist transfer decisions.

## Who Relies on the Output

Doctors, stroke teams, and hospital teams involved in acute stroke pathway
decisions.

## Action That Follows

Doctors use the AI-supported scan interpretation to inform treatment choices and
decide whether specialist transfer is needed.

## Human Review or Oversight

The published wording says the tools help doctors make choices, indicating that
clinicians remain the decision-makers. The public award page does not describe
the detailed review workflow.

## Impact Type

Individual patients in acute stroke pathways, with possible service-level impact
through faster specialist transfer and inter-hospital coordination.

## Published Governance or Safety Controls

The award page identifies the technology as a clinical decision aid. It does not
set out workflow-specific controls. Wider relevant controls may include
DCB0129/DCB0160 clinical safety assurance, clinical risk assessment, hazard
logs, clinical safety case reports, and Clinical Safety Officer oversight.

## BAT Reliance Point

Reliance appears to occur when scan interpretation output shapes treatment
choice, urgency, or transfer decisions in the acute pathway.

## BAT Questions

- Is the AI interpretation stored alongside the scan and clinical decision?
- What evidence shows how the doctor used or challenged the AI output?
- Are transfer decisions reconstructable across hospital handoffs?
- Is the timing of AI output, clinician review, and treatment decision recorded?
- What happens when AI output conflicts with clinician interpretation?

## Evidence Gaps for Follow-Up

- The public award page is brief and does not provide implementation detail.
- Local clinical protocols, audit logs, safety case material, and evaluation
  documents would be needed before this entry could support strong BAT findings.
