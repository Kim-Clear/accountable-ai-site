## Workflow Summary

Ambient scribing products convert clinician-patient conversations into
transcription and clinical summaries. The output may become part of the
consultation record and support downstream communication, documentation, and
care coordination.

## What the AI Does

The AI captures or processes consultation conversation and produces real-time or
near-real-time transcription, notes, or clinical summaries.

## Who Relies on the Output

Clinicians, care teams, NHS organisations, and downstream users of the clinical
record.

## Action That Follows

The output may be used to create or update the consultation record and support
follow-up communication, documentation, coding, referral, or care coordination.

## Human Review or Oversight

Human oversight is expected because clinicians and NHS bodies remain responsible
for procurement, governance, clinical safety, data protection, and use of the
resulting notes. The exact point and quality of clinician review should be
checked in local workflow material.

## Impact Type

Individual patients, because the output concerns their consultation record, and
possible group impact through documentation quality and downstream pathway use.

## Published Governance or Safety Controls

Published controls include NHS guidance for AI-enabled ambient scribing,
supplier registry expectations, clinical safety standards, information
governance and data protection requirements, and local procurement and
governance processes.

## BAT Reliance Point

Reliance appears to occur when the AI-generated note or summary is accepted into
the clinical record or used for downstream communication or action.

## BAT Questions

- What evidence shows that the clinician reviewed and accepted the AI-generated
  note?
- Are edits, omissions, uncertainty, and discarded suggestions retained?
- Can a later reviewer reconstruct what was AI-generated, clinician-edited, or
  independently authored?
- How are patient consent, transparency, data processing, and correction routes
  documented?
- What downstream decisions rely on the note once it becomes part of the record?

## Evidence Gaps for Follow-Up

- Local procurement and implementation processes will determine the actual
  review workflow.
- Supplier-specific behaviour, note editing logs, and clinical record integration
  details are needed for deeper BAT coding.
