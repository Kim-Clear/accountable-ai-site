# BAT Corpus

This folder contains source-grounded workflow entries used to test BAT
(Backward Accountability Traceability) against documented real-world material.

## BAT Corpus Rule

BAT corpus entries must be derived from real published workflows, policies,
operating models, guidance, case studies, deployments, evaluations, incident
reports, regulatory material, or other documented sources.

Invented or synthetic workflows may be used for teaching, demonstration,
exploration, or methodology development, but must not be used as evidence for
BAT validation, research findings, or public claims about methodology
performance.

Corpus entries should retain source references and enough context to allow
independent review of the originating material.

## Entry Status

- `source-extracted`: information has been extracted from published source
  material and is ready for BAT coding.
- `needs-source-review`: information is plausible from a secondary extraction,
  but should be checked against the originating material before being used as
  evidence.
- `bat-coded`: BAT observations have been applied to the entry.
- `reviewed`: the entry has been independently checked against the source.

## Suggested Entry Fields

- Workflow name
- Sector and setting
- Source references
- What the AI does
- Who relies on the output
- Action that follows
- Human review or oversight
- Individual, group, or aggregate impact
- Published governance or safety controls
- BAT reliance point
- BAT questions
- Evidence gaps
